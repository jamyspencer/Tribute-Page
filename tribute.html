Nelson Mandela

Early Life

  • 1918 - Born on 18 July in the village of Mvezo in Umtata.
  • 1939 - Attends university at Fort Hare.
  • 1941 - Flees an arranged marriage, travels to Johannesburg.
  • 1943 - Becomes involved with the ANC.
  • 1947 - Is appointed ANCYL secretary.
  • 1948 - Apartheid starts in South Africa.

Revolutionary Period

  • 1952 - ANC begans preparations for a joint Defense Campaign against apartheid.
  • 1952 - Mandela is imprisoned after adressing a crowd of 10,000 in Durban.
  • 1952 - Imprisoned again under the Suppression of Communism Act
    • Found guilty of "statutory communism"
    • given a 6 month ban from attending meetings.
    • also banned from speaking to more than one individual at a time.
  • 1953 - Public Safety Act passsed declaring martial law.
  • 1953 - Mandela works for two different law firms, ultimately starting a practice with a partner.
  • 1956 - Arrested and chraged with high treason.
  • 1960 - Mandela burns his pass.
  • 1961 - Mandela is found not-guilty of treason.
  • 1961 - Mandela co-founds Spear of the Nation.
  • 1962 - Arrested near Howick.
  • 1964 - Found guilty and sentenced to life in prison.

Later Life

  • 1990 - Released from prison.
  • 1991 - Negotiates a cease-fire.
  • 1992 - After escalating violence claims a number of lives Mandela recommits to negotions.
  • 1994 - General elections held.
  • 1995 - Urges black South Africans to get behind previously hated rugby team.
  • 1999 - Retired from politics.
  • 2003 - Inaugerates the Mandela Rhodes Foundation.
